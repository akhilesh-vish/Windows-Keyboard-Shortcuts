# 🔍 Search & Settings Shortcuts

Windows Search and the Settings app are two of the most powerful tools for finding applications, files, configuring your system, and troubleshooting problems. Learning these keyboard shortcuts allows you to locate what you need faster and access important system settings without navigating through multiple menus.

Whether you're searching for a document, adjusting display settings, or launching administrative tools, these shortcuts can save you valuable time every day.

> [!TIP]
> Instead of browsing through the Start menu, use **Windows Search** to find almost anything on your computer by simply typing its name.

| **Ctrl + Shift + Esc** | Open Task Manager directly.                                                          | Windows 10 / 11 |
| **Win + K**            | Open the Connect panel for wireless displays and supported audio devices.            | Windows 10 / 11 |

| **Win + H**            | Start Voice Typing (Dictation).                                                      | Windows 10 / 11 |

# 💡 Productivity Tips

* Use **Win + S** instead of manually browsing the Start menu. Simply type the first few letters of an application, file, or setting.
* Press **Win + R** to launch commonly used Windows tools like `cmd`, `powershell`, `control`, `regedit`, `services.msc`, or `msconfig`.
* If you're frequently adjusting system settings, keep the Settings app open and navigate using **Tab** and the **Arrow Keys** for faster keyboard-only operation.
* Enable **Clipboard History** to make copying and pasting multiple items much easier.
* Use **Voice Typing** (**Win + H**) when writing long notes, emails, or documents.

# 📝 Notes

> [!NOTE]

# 💭 Did You Know?

> 💡 **Windows Search can do more than find files.**
> It can quickly locate applications, settings, Control Panel items, documents, and even perform simple calculations and unit conversions directly from the search box.

---

> 💡 **The Run dialog is one of the fastest ways to access Windows tools.**
> Press **Win + R** and type commands such as `cmd`, `powershell`, `msconfig`, or `control` to launch them instantly.

---

> 💡 **You don't need a mouse to navigate Settings.**
> The **Tab**, **Shift + Tab**, **Arrow Keys**, and **Enter** keys allow you to move through nearly every page in the Settings app.

---

> 💡 **Clipboard History remembers multiple copied items.**
> Once enabled, **Win + V** lets you paste text, images, and other recently copied content without copying it again.
